I am currently 24 years old and I have lived in Omaha, NE since I was born.

I developed an interest in genetics and later in computer science during high school. One of my favorite high school teachers recommended that he should check out bioinformatics. After graduating from Millard West High School in May of 2010, I began my studies at the University of Nebraska at Omaha. I chose to major in bioinformatics, following the pre-medical track and taking honors courses. I graduated from the bioinformatics undergraduate program at the University of Nebraska at Omaha in May of 2014. There are special places in his heart for Ultimate Frisbee, Android, and Arduino.

I have been interested in human health and genetics for several years. One of the biggest areas of my interest is simply computer science. I took a liking to programming after I began to learn Java in 11th grade. Two of my other big interests are DIY electronics and neuroscience. One field of particular interest to me is the interfacing of mind and machine. What if prosthetics (of all sorts) could be developed that could interface closely with the brain and allow for very fine control?
